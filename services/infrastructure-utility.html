Service 05

Infrastructure & Utility Spatial Systems

Geospatial asset management systems for utility networks, linear infrastructure, and critical asset registries — enabling network tracing, condition mapping, and regulatory compliance for electricity, gas, water, and telecommunications operators.

Overview

Spatial Intelligence for Network Infrastructure

Utility and infrastructure networks — electricity distribution systems, gas transmission pipelines, water and wastewater networks, and telecommunications infrastructure — are fundamentally spatial assets. Their management, operation, and regulatory reporting depend on accurate, current, and functionally complete spatial records that reflect real-world network topology and asset condition.

Airfree Geospatial designs and deploys geospatial asset management systems specifically engineered for network infrastructure environments — where topological integrity, connectivity analysis, and integration with operational systems (SCADA, ERP, field mobility) are as important as spatial accuracy.

Problem Context

Utility operators frequently manage network assets across fragmented data environments — paper drawings, legacy CAD files, disconnected databases, and GIS systems that lack the topological correctness required for network analysis. The result: inability to trace network paths, identify isolation zones, or produce compliant regulatory asset reports without manual intervention.

A properly architected utility GIS resolves these operational limitations by providing a single, topologically correct, authoritative spatial record of the entire network — integrated with the operational and business systems that depend on it.

Solutions Provided

What We Deliver Under This Service

Network Asset Registry GIS

Structured geospatial asset registries for poles, conductors, substations, transformers, pipelines, valves, hydrants, meters, manholes, and telecommunications infrastructure. Feature class schema design with full attribute modelling for asset lifecycle management.

Network Topology & Tracing

Topologically correct network model construction enabling upstream and downstream network tracing, isolation zone identification, supply path analysis, and outage impact assessment. ArcGIS Utility Network and QGIS Network Analysis implementations.

AM/FM Integration

Automated Mapping/Facilities Management (AM/FM) integration linking GIS spatial records to ERP asset management systems, SCADA operational data, and field mobility platforms. Bi-directional data synchronisation and spatial attribute update workflows.

Regulatory Compliance Mapping

Spatial reporting for AER, AEMC, ESC, and state energy and water regulatory bodies. Safety management scheme maps, distribution zone maps, easement compliance mapping, and structured data exports in regulatory-specified formats.

Vegetation Encroachment Monitoring

Remote sensing-based vegetation clearance zone compliance monitoring for electricity transmission and distribution networks. Annual Sentinel-2 and UAV-based canopy encroachment detection mapped against registered easement extents.

As-Built Spatial Capture

UAV survey and field GPS capture of newly constructed or recently modified network assets. As-built GIS update workflows, spatial accuracy validation, and attribute population from works completion documentation.

Delivery Workflow

Utility GIS Implementation Process

01
Network Data Audit

Inventory of existing asset records, spatial data sources, and their completeness, accuracy, and format. Gap analysis against regulatory and operational requirements. Current system integration dependency mapping.

02
Schema & Topology Design

Network feature class schema design, topology rules, connectivity model, relationship classes, and domain/subtype configuration. Integration interface specification for connected ERP, SCADA, and field systems.

03
Data Migration & Topology Build

Migration of existing asset records, CAD conversion, field verification data collection, topology error resolution, and connectivity validation. GDA2020 coordinate transformation where required.

04
System Integration

AM/FM integration configuration, ERP attribute synchronisation, field mobile app setup, and GIS web service publication for operational viewers and regulatory reporting portals.

05
Acceptance & Operational Handover

Network trace validation testing, regulatory output verification, operational user acceptance testing, administrator training, and documentation handover.
