Remote Sensing & Geospatial AI Analytics

Multi-source satellite imagery analysis and machine learning classification pipelines for land use mapping, change detection, vegetation indices, and temporal analysis at any geographic scale.

Overview

Earth Observation Analytics for Enterprise Decision-Making

Remote sensing transforms orbital imagery into actionable spatial intelligence. Through the systematic analysis of multispectral, hyperspectral, and synthetic aperture radar (SAR) data, it is possible to characterise, monitor, and quantify land surface conditions at spatial and temporal scales impossible to achieve through field survey alone.

Airfree Geospatial applies rigorously validated remote sensing methodologies — including physical radiometric correction, supervised and unsupervised machine learning classification, and multi-temporal compositing — to produce analytically defensible outputs suitable for regulatory reporting, environmental accounting, and infrastructure planning.

Problem Context

Government agencies, environmental organisations, and large land managers need to understand what is happening across large land areas — often hundreds of thousands of hectares — on a regular basis. Ground-based monitoring cannot achieve this at scale. Periodic field survey generates point-in-time snapshots that miss dynamic change processes.

Satellite remote sensing, when correctly processed and interpreted, delivers spatially continuous, multi-temporal environmental intelligence across entire regions — enabling change detection, condition assessment, and predictive modelling that would otherwise be operationally infeasible.

Solutions Provided

What We Deliver Under This Service

Land Use / Land Cover Classification

Supervised classification of Sentinel-2 and Landsat multispectral imagery using Random Forest, Support Vector Machine, and convolutional neural network (CNN) algorithms. Class accuracy validated against independent reference datasets with confusion matrix reporting.

Vegetation Index Analysis

Time-series extraction and analysis of NDVI, EVI, NBR, NDWI, NDMI, and custom spectral indices. Seasonal decomposition, phenological trend analysis, and anomaly detection for agricultural, pastoral, and ecological monitoring applications.

Change Detection

Multi-temporal change detection using image differencing, post-classification comparison, and continuous change detection and classification (CCDC) algorithms. Vegetation clearing detection, urban expansion mapping, and land disturbance monitoring for regulatory compliance.

SAR & Interferometric Analysis

Synthetic aperture radar processing for surface deformation detection, subsidence monitoring, flood mapping, and soil moisture estimation. InSAR time-series analysis using Sentinel-1 GRD and SLC data processed through ESA SNAP and StaMPS workflows.

Google Earth Engine Pipelines

Large-area analysis pipelines using the Google Earth Engine cloud platform. Access to the full Sentinel, Landsat, MODIS, and SRTM archives. Planetary-scale compositing, classification, and time-series analysis without local compute constraints.

Geospatial AI & Deep Learning

Object detection, semantic segmentation, and instance segmentation of aerial and satellite imagery using convolutional neural networks (U-Net, ResNet, DeepLabV3+). Custom model training on labelled geospatial datasets for automated feature extraction.

Delivery Workflow

Remote Sensing Analysis Process

01
Data Source Selection & Acquisition

Imagery platform selection based on required resolution, revisit cycle, spectral bands, and cloud cover constraints. Archive search, scene selection, and download or API-based access configuration.

02
Pre-Processing & Atmospheric Correction

Radiometric calibration, atmospheric correction (Sen2Cor, ACOLITE, 6SV), cloud and shadow masking, geometric registration, and co-registration of multi-temporal scenes to a common reference.

03
Analysis & Classification

Feature extraction, training sample collection, classifier training, application, and result post-processing (sieving, generalisation, boundary smoothing). Intermediate outputs reviewed against reference data.

04
Accuracy Assessment

Independent validation using stratified random sampling. Confusion matrix, overall accuracy, producer and user accuracy, and Kappa coefficient calculation and reporting. Minimum 85% overall accuracy target for classification outputs.

05
Output Production & Reporting

Final raster and vector product generation, GIS-ready output in client-specified formats, and written technical report documenting methodology, data sources, processing steps, and accuracy results.
