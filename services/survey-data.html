Overview

Spatial Data Engineering in Support of Survey Operations

Survey data — whether derived from GNSS field capture, total station observations, LiDAR, photogrammetry, or historical records — requires rigorous data engineering before it can be reliably integrated into GIS environments, submitted to regulatory bodies, or used as the basis for infrastructure design and management decisions.

Airfree Geospatial provides the spatial data engineering and quality assurance capability that complements primary survey operations: coordinate reference system management, datum transformation, data format conversion, accuracy assessment, and structured QA/QC workflows that ensure spatial data meets the accuracy, completeness, and metadata standards required for its intended use.

Problem Context

Survey observations are collected in the field with precision — but the downstream journey from raw field data to a GIS-ready, metadata-complete, regulatorily compliant spatial dataset involves numerous transformation steps, each of which introduces opportunities for error, datum inconsistency, and format incompatibility.

Without systematic QA/QC processes and spatial data engineering expertise, organisations risk integrating inaccurate, incorrectly referenced, or inadequately documented spatial data into enterprise GIS systems — undermining the integrity of every subsequent analysis or decision based on that data.

Solutions Provided

What We Deliver Under This Service

Coordinate Reference System Management

Coordinate reference system identification, documentation, and management across multi-source spatial datasets. GDA94 to GDA2020 transformation, MGA zone assignment, vertical datum management (AHD, AVWS), and EPSG code validation for all delivered datasets.

Datum Transformation

Precise datum transformation between GDA94, GDA2020, WGS84, and local datum systems using NTv2 grids (GDA2020 National Transformation v3), PROJ library transformations, and transformation accuracy documentation for audit purposes.

Survey Data Integration & ETL

Integration of survey data from multiple sources and formats — GNSS CSV, total station DXF, LandXML, GeoPackage, Shapefile, and proprietary formats — into unified GIS data structures with consistent schema, CRS, and attribute formatting.

Spatial Accuracy Assessment

Independent positional accuracy assessment of spatial datasets against check points, reference datasets, or specification thresholds. RMSE calculation, horizontal and vertical accuracy reporting, and formal accuracy certification documentation.

Spatial QA/QC Workflow Design

Design and implementation of structured spatial data quality control workflows — covering completeness, logical consistency, positional accuracy, temporal accuracy, and thematic accuracy checks — for ongoing data custodianship operations.

Metadata Authoring

ISO 19115 / ISO 19139 compliant metadata authoring for all spatial datasets. ANZLIC Metadata Profile compliance, lineage documentation, quality report generation, and publication to spatial data catalogue systems (GeoNetwork, GeoNode).

Delivery Workflow

QA/QC & Data Engineering Process

01
Data Receipt & Inventory

Receipt, format identification, and initial integrity check of all input survey data. CRS identification, attribute inventory, and completeness assessment against specification or contract requirements.

02
Transformation & Integration

Datum and CRS transformation where required, format conversion, schema mapping, and integration of multi-source datasets into a unified spatial environment. Transformation parameters recorded in lineage documentation.

03
QA Checks

Systematic quality checks: geometry validity, duplicate feature detection, topology errors, attribute domain validation, mandatory field completeness, and positional accuracy verification against independent check points.

04
Error Reporting & Resolution

Structured error log generation with issue category, severity, and recommended resolution. Liaison with primary survey team for field-level corrections where required. Re-check after resolution.

05
Acceptance & Metadata Delivery

Final QA report, accuracy certificate, ISO 19115 metadata records, and GIS-ready spatial data package delivery in client-specified formats.
